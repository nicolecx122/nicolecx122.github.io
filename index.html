About

As a computational oceanographer and coastal ecosystem ecologist, my research is driven by a central question: How do land-sea interactions within the dynamic coastal transition zone, from the continental shelf to upland, shape and respond to climate change and other environmental stressors? I investigate tightly coupled physical and biogeochemical processes, with a focus on tidal dynamics, saltwater intrusion, carbon cycling, tidal wetlands, and benthic-pelagic coupling. I integrate process-based numerical modeling, machine learning, and data-driven analysis to investigate complex coastal systems across spatial and temporal scales. My work includes developing biogeochemical and ecological sub-models within the open-source 3D SCHISM platform, applying machine learning to enhance diagnostics and forecasting, and leveraging large observational datasets to uncover system patterns, test hypotheses, and inform both conceptual understanding and model development.
